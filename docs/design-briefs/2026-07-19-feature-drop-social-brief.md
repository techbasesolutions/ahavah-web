# Claude Design Brief — Ahavah feature-drop social posts (July update)

Hand this to claude.ai/design (project "Ahavah"). Campaign posts
announcing the July feature drop, sibling to "Ahavah First Match
Posts" and the Marriage Checklist post set — same brand system, same
export format. The member email has already gone out; these posts
carry the same story to Facebook and Instagram.

## The story (one campaign, four proofs)

Headline idea: the platform listens and ships. Four features went live
in one week, all from member feedback:

1. **See who viewed you** — every view of your profile, new ones
   flagged. (Lead feature: strongest curiosity hook for non-members.)
2. **You liked** — see everyone you liked or super liked, take a like
   back if you change your mind.
3. **Use my map view** — browse the map anywhere in the world and
   people from that area appear first in your feed.
4. **Nobody stays hidden** — distance and unanswered profile questions
   no longer hide anyone.

Angle for the public audience: this is matchmaking software being
built seriously, fast, in the open, for this community. "Built by
believers, shipped weekly" energy. Not feature-list dryness.

## Formats (match the Marriage post set)

- Square 1080x1080 (feed)
- Portrait 1080x1350 (feed)
- Story 1080x1920
- Decide: one hero post carrying all four features, or a hero plus a
  4-slide carousel (one feature per slide, square). Recommend the
  carousel; each feature gets one visual metaphor and one line.

## Visual direction

- Dark indigo world (#1A1340 / #0F0B1F), Plus Jakarta Sans, the
  sparkle mark, lime (#D7FF81) reserved for the single accent/CTA per
  frame, lavender for secondary accents.
- Prefer UI-fragment illustration over screenshots: recreate a single
  primitive per feature at poster scale (the eye-in-circle tile for
  views, a like card with the Super pill for You liked, the lavender
  map-lens chip for the map, a deck card for nobody-hidden). Poster
  scale means oversized, cropped, one per frame — the primitives are
  in the project (You Liked, Who Viewed You, Map Lens files).
- Keep faces from prior post sets if people are needed; no new
  member-identifiable imagery.

## Copy rules (hard requirements)

- NO em dashes anywhere. Sentence case.
- Avoid "dating app"; this is matchmaking for marriage.
- No mirrored-clause constructions ("X first, Y right after") — plain
  statements only.
- Never imply surveillance for the views feature: "see who has been
  looking" voice, not tracking.
- CTA: ahavah.app. Free during the beta may be mentioned.

## Deliverable

One self-contained HTML export ("Ahavah Feature Drop Posts") with all
frames on a canvas, same handoff format as "Ahavah First Match
Posts.html", plus a short design-decisions note. Export frames at
exact pixel dimensions listed above.
